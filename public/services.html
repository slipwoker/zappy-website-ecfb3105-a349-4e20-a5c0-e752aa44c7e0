Guiding Families Toward Practical Agreements

When families face legal transitions, the adversarial court system often deepens conflict and prolongs uncertainty. At Saldivar Family Law Mediation, we offer a different approach — one grounded in neutrality, respect, and lasting solutions. Our mediation practice helps couples and families navigate divorce, child custody, and asset division with clarity and compassion, preserving relationships and reducing the emotional and financial toll of litigation.

Mediation is not about winning or losing. It is about finding common ground. In a confidential, structured setting, we facilitate productive conversations that allow both parties to shape their own agreements — agreements that reflect their unique priorities and the best interests of their children. The result is a more peaceful transition and a foundation for healthier family dynamics moving forward.

We handle all aspects of family law mediation — from initial separation to final agreement — always prioritizing dignity, privacy, and the well-being of everyone involved.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Family Law Mediation

Clear answers to help you understand the mediation process and what to expect as you move toward resolution.

01

Who should attend mediation sessions?

Mediation typically involves the parties directly involved in the dispute — for example, both spouses in a divorce or both parents in a custody matter. In some cases, other family members or support persons may attend with the agreement of all parties. Your mediator will discuss who should be present during your initial consultation to ensure the sessions remain productive and balanced.

02

Do I need an attorney if I choose mediation?

While mediation is designed to help you reach agreements without courtroom litigation, you are always encouraged to consult with an independent attorney. Many clients choose to have a consulting attorney review proposed agreements before signing. The mediator remains neutral and does not provide legal advice to either party, which is why having your own legal counsel can provide valuable peace of mind.

03

Is everything discussed in mediation confidential?

Yes. Mediation is a confidential process under Texas law. Discussions, offers, and statements made during mediation generally cannot be used as evidence in court if the case does not settle. This confidentiality encourages open, honest communication and allows both parties to explore solutions without fear that their words will be used against them later.

04

How long does the mediation process typically take?

The timeline varies depending on the complexity of the issues and the willingness of both parties to collaborate. Some families reach a full agreement in a single session, while others benefit from two to four sessions spaced over several weeks. At Saldivar Family Law Mediation, we work at a pace that respects everyone's emotional readiness while keeping the process moving forward efficiently.

05

Can mediation work in high-conflict situations?

Absolutely. In fact, mediation can be especially valuable when emotions are running high. A skilled mediator is trained to manage difficult dynamics, ensure each person is heard, and keep conversations focused on practical solutions rather than past grievances. With the right structure and ground rules, even deeply contentious disputes can find a path to resolution through mediation.

06

What happens after we reach an agreement?

Once both parties agree on all terms, the mediator will prepare a detailed written summary of the agreements reached. This document can then be reviewed by your respective attorneys and, if appropriate, submitted to the court for approval and entry as a binding order. The goal is to leave mediation with a clear, actionable plan that brings lasting closure to your family law matter.
